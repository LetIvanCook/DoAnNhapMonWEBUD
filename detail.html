Sapa — The City in Clouds

Lào Cai Province 4.9 (2,400+ reviews) 3–5 days recommended
Overview

Where the Mountains Touch the Sky

Perched at 1,500 metres in Vietnam's northwestern highlands, Sapa is a world apart — a place where clouds settle into valleys every morning and the terraced hillsides turn gold each autumn. Once a French hill station, it has evolved into one of Southeast Asia's most celebrated trekking destinations, yet its soul remains rooted in the cultures of the Black Hmong, Red Dao, Tày, and Giáy peoples who have shaped these mountains for centuries.

The town sits just below Fansipan — Indochina's highest peak at 3,147 m — and serves as the gateway to the dramatic Muong Hoa Valley, a UNESCO-recognised cultural landscape of layered rice paddies that appears almost impossibly beautiful at harvest time in September and October.
